# NoHum Copyable Product Thesis

We do not look for generic internet businesses or interesting founder stories.

We look for `copyable default-path software products`: products that can be built quickly, taken to market, and pushed to first revenue without heavy infrastructure, enterprise sales, or expensive acquisition.

## Hard Gates

- The product has one clear primary audience.
- The product has one clear problem and understandable JTBD.
- The problem recurs often enough to support subscription or repeat payment.
- The market has at least `3` live direct competitors.
- Competitors and/or the product itself show understandable monetization and a visible path to first payment.
- The audience already pays for solving this job directly or through close substitutes.
- The product has clear value delta relative to the status quo: with the product, the user wins on time, money, risk, cognitive load, emotional load, or some combination of these.
- There is a realistic and not-too-expensive path to reach the audience.
- The product can be built quickly without heavy technical scaffolding.
- Switching friction between the current way of solving the job and the new product is not fatally high.
- There is a plausible path to `$5k MRR` in a reasonable time horizon.

## Strong Positive Signals

- The market already looks alive rather than imagined: real money, traffic, subscriptions, or customers are visible.
- Demand sources are legible: search, competitor traffic, community, marketplace, content, direct, or another believable acquisition path.
- The product has a plausible conversion story: traffic and paying behavior do not imply obviously broken economics.
- The product is narrow enough to reproduce as `default-path software`, not a custom implementation project.
- The buyer, use case, and value are understandable without a long educational sales motion.

## Explicit Exclusions

- Service-heavy business.
- Agency model.
- Marketplace-heavy model.
- Enterprise-sales-heavy motion.
- Businesses where first payment requires long implementation, procurement, or integration projects.
- Businesses where most of the value lives in manual service rather than in the product.
- Businesses that cannot be shipped quickly in NoHum's canonical stack.

## Operating Interpretation For Idea Scout

`Idea Scout` uses this thesis as a `search-and-reject heuristic`.

At the sourcing stage, the scout should prefer candidates that already look like:

- narrow software products
- understandable buyer and use case
- visible monetization
- visible traffic or market activity
- plausible `default-path` build shape

At the sourcing stage, the scout should reject or heavily downgrade candidates that already look like:

- service-heavy
- agency-shaped
- marketplace-shaped
- enterprise-procurement-heavy
- too broad or too infrastructure-heavy to copy quickly

`Idea Scout` does **not** prove the full thesis. It only finds candidates that look worth shortlist review and returns them in a structured sourcing batch.

Question answered by the scout:

`Does this candidate look enough like a copyable NoHum product to deserve shortlist review?`

## Operating Interpretation For Research Lead

`Research Lead` uses this thesis as the `intake decision framework`.

At the intake boundary, the lead should decide whether a candidate deserves a canonical intake card and specialist time.

The lead should use the thesis to answer:

- is the audience clear enough?
- is the problem clear enough?
- does the shape look recurring rather than one-off?
- does monetization look real rather than accidental?
- does the business look copyable rather than service-led or enterprise-led?
- is there enough signal to justify deeper research?

`Research Lead` does **not** treat the sourcing batch as proof that the thesis is satisfied. The sourcing batch is only shortlist input.

Question answered by the lead:

`Does this candidate deserve canonical intake and specialist research as a potential copyable NoHum product?`

## Role Split

- `Idea Scout` finds promising candidates and rejects obvious non-fit.
- `Research Lead` decides who gets intake.
- Specialists prove the deeper market, demand, revenue, and competition claims after intake.
