# Traffic Interpretation Bands

Traffic is not interpreted the same way across product shapes.

## Archetype 1: Broad SMB Self-Serve Web App

- strong: `>= 20k` estimated visits per month or equivalent multi-source usage proof
- medium: `5k-20k`
- weak: `< 5k`

Interpretation:

- This archetype usually depends on repeated search and casual discovery.
- Low traffic is a warning unless review or payment proof is unusually strong.

## Archetype 2: Niche B2B Workflow Tool

- strong: `>= 3k` estimated visits per month
- medium: `1k-3k`
- weak: `< 1k`

Interpretation:

- Lower traffic can still be good if the buyer is narrow and intent is obvious.
- Review recency, visible pricing, and public customer proof matter more here.

## Archetype 3: Plugin, Add-On, or Ecosystem Tool

- strong: clear install, ranking, review, or integration footprint even with modest site traffic
- medium: modest footprint with recent usage proof
- weak: thin footprint and no adoption proof

Interpretation:

- Site traffic can understate real demand.
- Marketplace proof can count more than homepage traffic.

## Global Rules

- One traffic tool alone rarely deserves `high` confidence.
- Falling traffic is not automatically fatal if monetization and buyer intent are still strong.
- Unknown traffic is acceptable only when another usage proxy clearly covers the same claim.
- Zero traffic plus zero usage proof fails the `traffic or usage` demand class.

## What Counts As Good Traffic

Good traffic means:

- enough discovery to support the expected pricing and path to first payment
- not obviously inflated by unrelated audiences
- consistent with the category and buyer size
- supported by at least one additional usage or buyer signal
